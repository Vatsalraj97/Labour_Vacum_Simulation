# Labour Vacuum Simulator — US Manufacturing 2025–2033

A PyQt6 desktop simulation modelling the US manufacturing labour market from 2025 to 2033. Workers are represented as individual agents (balls) distributed across 10 skill-tier tubes, where labour shortage manifests as a physical vacuum — the gap between what the market demands and what the workforce can actually deliver. The simulation runs in quarterly steps, tracking skill growth, graduation between tiers, retirement and exit dynamics, policy shocks, reshoring demand, and a market placement engine with realistic hiring barriers. An animated canvas visualises tube fill levels, talent pool activity, and tier-by-tier vacuum in real time. All parameters are adjustable via a dockable controls panel, with Monte Carlo support for uncertainty analysis across 500 runs.
